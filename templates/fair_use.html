What is Fair Use?

Fair use is a legal doctrine that promotes freedom of expression by permitting the unlicensed use of copyright-protected works in certain circumstances.

Four Factors of Fair Use

1. Purpose and Character of Use

Our service is intended for educational, research, and personal use. Non-commercial, transformative uses are more likely to be considered fair use.

2. Nature of the Copyrighted Work

Factual works are more likely to qualify for fair use than creative works. Published works are more likely to qualify than unpublished works.

3. Amount and Substantiality

Using small portions of a work is more likely to be fair use than using the entire work. However, even small portions may infringe if they constitute the "heart" of the work.

4. Effect on the Potential Market

Uses that don't harm the copyright owner's ability to profit from their work are more likely to be fair use.

Appropriate Uses

Examples of potentially fair uses include:

  • Educational research and scholarship
  • News reporting and commentary
  • Personal archiving of legally obtained content
  • Criticism and review
  • Parody and transformation

User Responsibility

Users are responsible for:

  • Determining whether their use qualifies as fair use
  • Obtaining permission when fair use does not apply
  • Ensuring compliance with local copyright laws
  • Using downloaded content responsibly and legally
